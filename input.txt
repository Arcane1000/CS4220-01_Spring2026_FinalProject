My Game Console Collector's List

My Rules for my Gaming Collection.

#1: Backwards Compatibility.
- The ability of older games and hardware to be played on newer consoles.
- There wouldn't be a need to buy every console, just a select few. Saves money that way.
- Example: The Nintendo Wii U can play Wii games with Wii Remotes.

#2: Region Lock
- The restriction of games from one region to be played on a console of another.
- Region Free means the games are playable on a console no matter the region.
- Example: The Nintendo DS is Region-Free, so it can play games of any region:
    - American, Japan, Europe, etc.

#3: Nostalgia
- The connection/sentiment associated with the console, game titles, and experiences.
- Collections can be built by memories from childhood and favorite games and titles.
- Example: I love the Smash Bros. games, so I plant o get consoles compatible with all game titles for Smash.

#4: Multiplayer
- Games or Consoles that allow two or more players to play together, locally or online.
- Best for family, friends, gatherings, game nights, parties, etc.
- Example: Multiple Controllers for home consoles, Multiple Consoles for Handhelds.

#5: Pokemon
- A Game series where you collect Pokemon to complete the Pokedex.
- Every Generation releases two versions of the game. Impossible to complete one without the other.
- Example: For handhelds, multiple consoles required for trading and battling.

#6: Variety
- Multiple versions, colors, and models of the console family.
- Adds uniqueness and diversity to my collection.
- Example: My 3DS Collection has consoles of different versions. (3DS XL, 2DS, New 3DS XL, New 2DS XL)


CONSOLE DETAILS & CHECKLIST

Nintendo (Home):

Nintendo Entertainment System (NES)
- Released in North America, 1985. Best-known for bringing the video game industry back up after the crash in 1983.
- Most of Nintendo's Iconic Game Franchises and Titles started on the NES: Super Mario Bros., Duck Hunt, The Legend of Zelda, Metroid, Dragon Quest, Final Fantasy.
- Console is on my list.
  - Nintendo Entertainment System (NES-001, White & Gray)

Super NES (SNES)
- Released in North America, 1991. Best-known for expanding on the NES's success with improved graphics, sound, and controller design.
